i Power threshold (ρ₀)

The minimum ICC value you need to demonstrate — the bar your study must clear.

Power shown above is the probability that the lower bound of your CI stays above ρ₀, letting you claim "agreement is at least ρ₀."

For a meaningful calculation, set ρ₀ below your assumed ρ — e.g., ρ = 0.70 with ρ₀ = 0.50 asks "if agreement is really 0.70, how often can I prove it's above 0.50?"

i Joint-confidence mode

When you report several ICCs (here, 3 rater pairs × the number of criteria), each test carries its own error rate. Without adjustment, the chance that at least one shows a spuriously favourable result grows with the count.

Switching this on applies a Bonferroni correction: α is divided by the number of ICCs, so each individual test must clear a higher bar.

The result: wider CIs and lower per-ICC power, but the confidence level you set now applies to the joint claim (e.g., "all 9 ICCs exceed ρ₀") rather than to each ICC in isolation.

Notes & methodology

What N means

N is the number of participants sampled, not the number of responses. Each sampled participant contributes all R of their responses, so the effective ICC sample is N × R. Sliding N up adds whole batches of R responses at a time.

Data-generating model

Each response is drawn as y_ij = μ + s·(α_i + e_ij) with Var(α) = ρ, Var(e) = 1 − ρ. The latent score is shifted to the centre of your rating scale, scaled so that ±2 SD covers the range, then rounded to integer and clipped to the scale bounds — mimicking real 1–10 ratings, including the slight ICC attenuation from discretization.

Estimator and CI

ICC is computed as (MS_between − MS_within) / (MS_between + MS_within) — one-way random-effects, ICC(1,1). The CI uses the Fisher-z transform W = ½·log((1+r)/(1−r)) with SE(W) ≈ 1/√(N_eff − 2) · √((Pop − N_eff) / (Pop − 1)). The second factor is the finite-population correction (matters when your sample is a non-trivial fraction of the pool).

Required-N formula

Bonett (2002): n₀ ≈ 1 + 8z²(1−ρ)²(1+ρ)² / (2w²) for half-width w. With FPC and converted back to participants, N = ⌈n₀·Pop / (Pop + n₀ − 1) / R⌉.

Multiple ICCs

With 3 rater pairs (H1–H2, AI–H1, AI–H2) × C criteria, you'll report 3C separate ICCs. Joint-confidence mode applies Bonferroni: divides α by 3C, so each individual ICC must clear a higher bar. Wider CIs, lower per-ICC power, valid joint claim.

Clustering

Responses from the same participant tend to be correlated. The simulator treats responses as independent for ICC computation, which is the standard inter-rater convention but slightly understates variance if within-participant agreement is high. If that's a concern, multiply N by the design effect 1 + (R−1)·ρ_intra as a buffer.

Discrete 1–10 ratings

The simulator already rounds to your chosen scale. The discretization gap to a continuous ICC is small for ρ ≲ 0.9; above that, add a 10–15% buffer to N.
